About
MHRD policies and decisions are generally taken for the benefits of students - currently studying in one of the institutions under its purview and also the aspirants. In the recent times there has been a sea of changes in the policies, for example the format of the JEE, the course structure, the salaries of the PhD students and the number of IITs, to name a few.

Most of these decisions were taken by the MHRD themselves, whereas some were taken after requests (and in some case even protests) from the students. In many cases the one thing that was missing was consultation from the people affected by it. The policies are taken by people who are brilliant in their field and who know all the technicalities of the issues. But, a student perspective would always be better.

Our Application
We develop an interface connecting the students, the student representatives, Directors and Deans of all government institutions (IITs, NITs etc) with the MHRD. There are two kinds of interactions between the MHRD and the institutions, firstly when the MHRD, while proposing a policy change or creation can post the same on the forum and everyone else on that forum can discuss how that policy would be seen from an institutes perspective.

Moreover, the students have some issues, so they can post the same on the forum. These issues will be within an institution, within a subset of all institutions or for all institutions. If the student representatives feel that this issue is big enough to be elevated further, they can elevate the issue and it can be seen by the MHRD and the Directors and Deans of institutions. They can reply to that issue with an existing policy or can draft a new one base on it. This will ensure that the decisions taken by the ministry are transparent and democratic.

Further, our interface would ensure that the more pressing issues are seen first. People will be authenticated via the IDs of their institutions so it’ll ensure that there is no spam. Since our portal doesn’t show all discussions to the HRD and only the elevated issues, this will ensure that people can openly talk about their problems.
